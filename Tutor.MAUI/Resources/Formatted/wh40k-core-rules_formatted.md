## Core Rules

> **‘We are beset on all sides by vile predatory aliens and sedition gnaws at us from within; in this dark hour the best we can do is look to our wargear and pray to our gods.’**  
> — Skolak a’Trellar IV, Imperial Commander

Welcome to the **Warhammer 40,000 Core Rules**! This guide contains everything you need to wage glorious battles across the war-torn galaxy of the **41st Millennium**.

**Warhammer 40,000** is a tabletop wargame where players command armies of **Citadel miniatures** to defeat their opponents through **skill, tactics, and luck**. Storytelling is central, with rules designed to bring epic conflicts to life in the grim darkness of the far future.

The game's purpose is to create an enjoyable shared experience, testing tactical skills while admiring amazing miniatures clashing on fantastic battlefields. **Good sportsmanship** and **politeness** are fundamental.

Games are won by scoring more **Victory points** than your opponent by completing various objectives such as recovering relics, capturing strongholds, or eliminating enemy **Warlords**.

The game is played in **battle rounds**, divided into phases where players move, shoot, and fight with their miniatures.

When unsure about a rule during play, discuss with your opponent to find the most sensible or fun solution. If no consensus is reached, roll off—the highest roll decides.

## Core Concepts (pg 5-9)

### Overview
This section introduces **essential rules terms and concepts** that underpin every Warhammer 40,000 battle.

- **Engagement Range:** Zone of threat a model presents to enemies; within 1" horizontally and 5" vertically of enemy models.
- Models cannot be set up or end moves within Engagement Range of enemies.
- **Battlefield:** Rectangular surfaces (tables, floors) selected based on mission guidance.
- **Terrain Features:** Objects like ruins and wreckage that can affect movement and line of sight.
- Distances are measured in **inches (")** and between closest model bases or parts.
- **Hints and Tips:** Advice on gameplay, model handling, and dice-rolling (not rules).
- Page numbers are referenced within stylized triangles unique to the Core Rules.

## Missions (pg 57-60)

### General

Before playing, select a **mission**. Missions dictate how to muster armies, create the battlefield, deploy forces, any special rules, and victory conditions.

### Armies
- Each player commands an **army** of **models** (Citadel miniatures).
- Mission informs army size.
- **Army:** All models under your command.

### Units
- Models move and fight in **units** of one or more models from the same datasheet.
- Units/Models from the same army are **friendly**; from others are **enemy**.
- Rules applying to 'models' or 'units' apply to all unless stated.

- **Unit:** One or more models from a datasheet.
- **Friendly Models/Units:** From your army.
- **Enemy Models/Units:** From opponents.

### Datasheets
- Contain unit characteristics and abilities.
- Find more details on pages 37-38.

### Keywords
- Datasheets list keywords separated into **Faction** keywords and others.
- Keywords help define unit interactions and rule applicability (e.g., Infantry).
- Keywords are always in **Keyword Bold** and linked to relevant rules.

## Core Concepts: Key Terms

### Unit Coherency
- Units with multiple models must be grouped:
  - Units of 2-6 models: each model within **2" horizontally** and **5" vertically** of at least one other model.
  - Units of 7+ models: within 2" horizontally and 5" vertically of at least **two** other models.
- Failure to maintain coherency leads to model destruction or movement restriction.
- At turn end, units out of coherency must remove models until coherency is restored.

### Engagement Range
- Within **1" horizontally** and **5" vertically** of enemy models.
- Models cannot be set up or end moves within enemy Engagement Range.
- Violation results in model destruction.

### Battlefield and Terrain Features
- Battles fought on **rectangular battlefields**.
- **Terrain features:** Scenery models that block/affect movement and visibility.
- Terrain setup follows mission or can be customized.
- Models can move over terrain features 2" or less tall; taller features require climbing (vertical distance counted).
- Models cannot move through terrain features.

### Measuring Distances
- Distances measured in inches ("), between closest points of model bases or parts.
- 'Within' a distance means **≤ specified distance**.

## Hints and Tips

### Wobbly Models
- If terrain causes models to precariously balance, players can agree to keep the model in a safer position with a known ‘actual’ location.
- Visible and cover checks require the model to be physically placed correctly if targeted.

## Determining Visibility

Warhammer 40,000 uses **true line of sight**:

- **Model Visible:** Any part of a model visible to any part of the observer.
- **Unit Visible:** If at least one model in the unit is visible.
- **Model Fully Visible:** Every facing part visible without obstruction.
- **Unit Fully Visible:** All models fully visible.
- Observing models can see through other models in the same unit.
- A model’s base counts as part of the model for visibility.

## Dice (pg 9)

- Use six-sided dice (D6).  
- **D3** rolls made by rolling a D6 and halving (rounded up).
  
### Re-rolls
- Allowed only once per dice roll, before modifiers.
- Multi-dice totals (2D6, 3D6, etc.) require re-rolling all dice.
- Unmodified dice are post re-roll/pre modifier.

### Roll-offs
- Both players roll 1D6; highest wins.
- Ties re-roll.
- No re-rolls or modifiers allowed in roll-offs.

### Sequencing
- Player whose turn it is chooses order of simultaneous effects during battle.
- Outside battle or at round start/end, roll off for order.

## The Battle Round (pg 10-36)

Each battle round consists of both players taking a turn, with the same player always taking the first turn.

### Turn Phases:
1. **Command Phase**
2. **Movement Phase**
3. **Shooting Phase**
4. **Charge Phase**
5. **Fight Phase**

Players alternate until the battle ends.

---

## Command Phase (pg 11-12)

### 1. Command
- Both players gain **1 Command Point (CP)**.
- Resolve any other Command phase effects.

### 2. Battle-Shock
- For units **Below Half-strength**, roll **2D6** vs unit's **Leadership (Ld)**.
- Pass = unit is okay.
- Fail = unit is **Battle-shocked** until your next Command phase.
  
**Battle-shocked units:**
- Objective Control = 0.
- Must take Desperate Escape tests if they Fall Back.
- Cannot be affected by Stratagems.

### Additional Terms:
- **Starting Strength:** Number of models at army muster.
- **Below Half-strength:** Less than half Starting Strength models remain.
- **Destroyed:** When all models in a unit are removed.
- **Attached Units:** Leaders attached to Bodyguards form one unit with combined Starting Strength.

---

## Movement Phase (pg 12-16)

### 1. Move Units
- Select each unit to move.
- If no enemy within Engagement Range: Normal move, Advance, or Remain Stationary.
- If within Engagement Range: can only Fall Back or Remain Stationary.

### Move Types:
- **Normal Move:** Models move ≤ Move (M) characteristic; cannot enter enemy Engagement Range.
- **Advance Move:** Roll 1D6, add to M; cannot enter enemy Engagement Range; cannot shoot or charge this turn.
- **Fall Back Move:** Models move ≤ M; may move through enemy models (Desperate Escape tests apply); cannot shoot or charge.

### Desperate Escape Test:
- Roll 1D6 per model moving through enemies.
- For rolls 1-2, one model destroyed.

### 2. Reinforcements
- Reserves units can arrive in the Reinforcements step.
- Must set up per their special arrival rules.
- Count as having made a Normal move this turn.

## Transports (pg 16-18)

### Transport Capacity
- Datasheets list max friendly models carried.
- Units can start embarked.

### Embark
- Units can embark after moves if ending within **3"** of the Transport.
- Cannot embark and disembark in the same phase.
- Remove embarked unit from battlefield.

### Disembark
- Units can disembark if their Transport didn't Advance or Fall Back that phase.
- Must be placed wholly within 3" of Transport and not within enemy Engagement Range.
- Units may move or act based on when they disembarked relative to Transport’s move.
- Units cannot disembark if Transport Advanced or Fell Back.

### Firing Deck
- Some Transports have firing hatches.
- When Transport shoots, it can use weapons from embarked models (up to x models).

### Destroyed Transports
- Units must disembark before removal.
- Roll 1D6 per model disembarking:
  - On 1: 1 mortal wound.
- Disembarking unit is Battle-shocked until next Command phase.
- Emergency Disembarkation if setup conditions cannot be met—set up to 6" away, take more mortal wounds.

## Shooting Phase (pg 19-20)

### Eligibility
- Units that Advanced or Fell Back can’t shoot (except with Assault or Pistol weapons).
- Units within enemy Engagement Range can’t shoot.

### Shooting Sequence
1. **Select eligible units one at a time to shoot.**
2. **Select targets:** Must be visible and within weapon range.
3. **Make ranged attacks:** Each weapon makes a number of attacks equal to its **Attacks (A)** value.

### Special Cases
- Units can select multiple target units.
- Weapons cannot split attacks across targets.
- Monsters and Vehicles can shoot even if within enemy Engagement Range but have -1 to Hit rolls.
- Pistols allow shooting within Engagement Range, but only Pistols may be used.

---

## Making Attacks (pg 21-24)

Attack steps executed for each attack:

1. **Hit Roll**
   - Roll 1D6; success if ≥ **Ballistic Skill (BS)** for ranged or **Weapon Skill (WS)** for melee.
   - 6 unmodified always hits (**Critical Hit**).
   - 1 unmodified always fails.
   - Hit rolls modified by max ±1.

2. **Wound Roll**
   - Roll 1D6; compare **Strength (S)** of attack vs target’s **Toughness (T)**:
     
     | S vs T Condition                    | Needed on D6  |
     |-----------------------------------|---------------|
     | Strength ≥ 2× Toughness            | 2+            |
     | Strength > Toughness               | 3+            |
     | Strength = Toughness               | 4+            |
     | Strength < Toughness               | 5+            |
     | Strength ≤ ½ Toughness             | 6+            |

   - 6 unmodified always wounds (**Critical Wound**).
   - 1 unmodified always fails.
   - Wound rolls modified by max ±1.

3. **Allocate Attack**
   - Controller of target unit assigns hits to models.
   - If a model is already damaged or allocated attacks this phase, new wounds must be assigned to it first.

4. **Saving Throw**
   - Target player rolls 1D6 adjusted by attack’s **Armour Penetration (AP)** (- modifies roll).
   - Success if roll ≥ model’s **Save (Sv)**.
   - 1 unmodified always fails.
   - Save roll can be improved max +1.
   - May use **invulnerable saves** instead (never modified by AP).

5. **Inflict Damage**
   - Damage equals weapon’s **Damage (D)** characteristic.
   - Models lose wounds equal to damage.
   - Models destroyed if wounds ≤ 0.
   - Excess damage is lost.

### Mortal Wounds
- Inflict damage without wound rolls or saving throws.
- Allocate damage one at a time until exhausted or target destroyed.
- Excess damage can be allocated to other models.

## Special Abilities (pg 20-23)

### Stealth
- Units with Stealth subtract 1 from each incoming ranged Hit roll.

### Feel No Pain x+
- Damage potentially ignored by rolling 1D6 per damage; if result ≥ x, wound ignored.

### Deadly Demise x
- When model destroyed, roll 1D6: on 6, each unit within 6" suffers x mortal wounds.

## Weapon Abilities (pg 25-28)

- **Assault:** Can shoot if unit Advanced, but only Assault weapons.
- **Rapid Fire X:** Attacks increase by X if target within half range.
- **Ignores Cover:** Target cannot benefit from Cover against that attack.
- **Twin-linked:** Re-roll Wound rolls.
- **Pistol:** Can shoot in enemy Engagement Range; only Pistols may be used.
- **Torrent:** Attacks automatically hit.
- **Lethal Hits:** Critical Hits automatically wound.
- **Lance:** +1 to Wound rolls if bearer Charged.
- **Indirect Fire:** Can target invisible units, with Hit roll penalty and Cover applied if no targets visible.
- **Precision:** Can allocate attacks to Character models in Attached units if visible.
- **Blast:** Random attacks, +1 per 5 models; cannot target units within friendly Engagement Range.
- **Melta X:** +X Damage when target within half range.
- **Heavy:** +1 to Hit if bearer’s unit Remained Stationary.
- **Hazardous:** After shooting/fighting, roll D6 per Hazardous weapon; on 1, appropriate model destroyed or suffers mortal wounds.
- **Devastating Wounds:** Critical Wound causes mortal wounds equal to Damage, no allocation.
- **Sustained Hits X:** Critical Hit causes X additional hits.
- **Extra Attacks:** Bearer makes attacks with this weapon in addition to chosen weapon.
- **Anti-Keyword X+:** Against targets with matching keyword, unmodified Wound roll of X+ scores Critical Wound.

## Charge Phase (pg 29-30)

### Eligibility
- Units within 12" of enemy units.
- Excludes units that Advanced, Fell Back, are Aircraft, or already in Engagement Range.

### Charging Procedure
1. **Select eligible units and targets (one or more enemy units within 12").**
2. **Roll 2D6 for Charge distance.**
3. **If distance allows:**
   - Move units into Engagement Range of all selected targets,
   - Maintain Unit Coherency,
   - Must not move within Engagement Range of non-target enemy units.
4. **If not possible, charge fails, no move.**

Charged units gain **Fights First** ability until turn end.

### Charging Over Terrain
- Models can move over terrain features ≤ 2" tall.
- Must count vertical distances climbing taller features.
- Cannot end charge mid-climb.

### Flying Models
- Can move over other models.
- Charge distance measured through air when starting/ending on terrain features.

## Fight Phase (pg 31-36)

Two steps where players alternate selecting eligible units to fight.

### Eligibility
- Units within Engagement Range of enemy units.
- Units that Charged this turn.

### Steps
1. **Fights First:** All eligible units with **Fights First** ability fight.
2. **Remaining Combats:** All other eligible units fight, including newly eligible units.

### Fight Sequence For A Unit
1. **Pile In:** Move each model up to 3" closer to nearest enemy model, ending in base contact if possible.
2. **Make Melee Attacks:**  
   - Models fight if within Engagement Range or in base-to-base contact with models that are.
   - Choose one melee weapon per model.
   - Select enemy target units for all attacks before resolving.
3. **Consolidate:** Move models up to 3" closer to closest enemy or objective marker, maintaining Unit Coherency.

No model may fight more than once per Fight phase.

## Datasheets (pg 37-39)

Datasheets detail unit information:

1. **Datasheet Name**
2. **Profiles:**  
   - Move (M)  
   - Toughness (T)  
   - Save (Sv)  
   - Wounds (W)  
   - Leadership (Ld)  
   - Objective Control (OC)
3. **Abilities:** Special rules, aura effects, wargear abilities.
4. **Weapons:** Characteristics listed for ranged and melee.
5. **Keywords:** Faction and unit keywords.
6. **Unit Composition:** Number and type of models; default weapons/wargear.
7. **Wargear Options:** Alternate equipment for models.

### Special Notes

- **Leadership Tests:** Roll 2D6 vs best Leadership in unit.
- **Random Characteristics:** Indicated by dice notation (e.g., 2D6").
- **Aura Abilities:** Affect units within range once.
- **Psychic Weapons/Abilities:** Used only by Psykers; wounds from these are Psychic Attacks.
- **Attached Units:** Leaders attach to Bodyguards forming single units; attacks allocated differently.

## Deployment Abilities (pg 39)

- **Deep Strike:** Set units in Reserves; can arrive on battlefield >9" from enemies in Reinforcement phase.
- **Scouts x":** Move up to x" before first turn begins.
- **Infiltrators:** Set up anywhere >9" away from enemy deployment and models.
- **Leader:** Characters that can attach to Bodyguards to form Attached units.

## Stratagems (pg 40-42)

### General
- Spend **Command Points (CP)** to activate.
- Cannot use same Stratagem more than once per phase.
- CP cost reduces total available CP.

### Categories
- Battle Tactic: Boost unit efficacy.
- Epic Deed: Individual heroics.
- Strategic Ploy: Tactical advantages.
- Wargear: Special equipment effects.

### Examples
- **Command Re-roll (1CP):** Re-roll a single dice roll.
- **Counter-Offensive (2CP):** After enemy unit fights, your unit fights next.
- **Epic Challenge (1CP):** Character unit's melee attacks gain [PRECISION].
- **Insane Bravery (1CP):** Pass failed Battle-shock test.
- **Grenade (1CP):** Grenades unit inflicts mortal wounds.
- **Tank Shock (1CP):** Vehicle charge inflicts mortal wounds.
- **Rapid Ingress (1CP):** Unit in Reserves arrives early.
- **Fire Overwatch (1CP):** Shoot in opponent’s Movement or Charge phase.
- **Go To Ground (1CP):** Infantry gains 6+ invulnerable save and Cover.
- **Smokescreen (1CP):** Smoke unit gains Cover and Stealth.
- **Heroic Intervention (2CP):** Charge outside your Charge phase.

## Strategic Reserves (pg 43)

- Units kept off battlefield until later.
- Units in **Strategic Reserves** before battle max 25% points of army.
- Arrive from second battle round onward in Reinforcements step.
- Must be placed near battlefield edges, avoiding enemy deployment zones early.
- Cannot be placed closer than 9" to enemy models.
- Units not arriving count as destroyed.

---

## Terrain Features (pg 44-48)

### General
- Affect movement and line of sight.
- Models can move over/around terrain features.
- **Benefit of Cover:** +1 to saving throws except against AP 0 and Saves 3+ or better.
- Multiple cover instances do not stack.

### Types

- **Craters and Rubble:**  
  - Movement: Can move over.  
  - Visibility: Normal.  
  - Cover: Infantry models wholly on feature gain Cover.

- **Barricades and Fuel Pipes:**  
  - Movement: Can move up/down, cannot set up/end move on.  
  - Engagement: Charge and Fight phases allow attacks through barrier if within 2".  
  - Cover: Infantry units within 3" gain Cover if obscured.

- **Battlefield Debris and Statuary:**  
  - Movement: Can move up/down, cannot set up/end move on.  
  - Visibility: Normal.  
  - Cover: Models obscured gain Cover.

- **Hills, Industrial Structures, Sealed Buildings, Armoured Containers:**  
  - Movement: Can be set up or end moves on top if not overhanging.  
  - Visibility: Normal.  
  - Cover: Same as above.

- **Woods:**  
  - Footprint agreed by players.  
  - Movement: Can move over.  
  - Visibility: Models within not fully visible; have Cover.  
  - Exceptions: Aircraft and Towering models ignore these restrictions.

- **Ruins:**  
  - Footprint agreed by players.  
  - Plunging Fire: Models ≥6" vertically gain +1 AP when shooting ground-level units.  
  - Movement: Infantry and Beasts move through; others only on ground floor.  
  - Visibility: Blocks sight except for Aircraft/Towering units above.  
  - Cover: Models inside or obscured gain Cover.

## Example Battlefields (pg 49-52)

- Battlezones composed of boards ~22" x 30".
- Balanced terrain placement avoids favoring shooting or melee armies.
- Matched play battlefields have terrain blocking line of sight near center.
- Narrative play battlefields can be thematic with varying terrain density.

## Aircraft (pg 53-54)

### Deployment
- Must start in Reserves; treated as Strategic Reserves.
- Dedicated Transports can carry embarked units only if also in Reserves.

### Movement
- Can ONLY make Normal moves.
- Move straight forward at least 20".
- Then pivot up to 90°.
- Crossing battlefield edge or failing min move sends unit into Strategic Reserves for next turn.

### Hover Mode
- Declared at battle formation.
- Model loses Aircraft keyword and associated rules.
- Move changes to 20".
- Can be deployed on battlefield or in Strategic Reserves normally.

### Interaction
- Units only threatened by enemy Aircraft can Normal or Advance move.
- Models can move over Aircraft and move within Engagement Range but not end moves in Engagement Range of Aircraft.
- Aircraft cannot Charge or use Pile In/Consolidate moves.
- Only units that can Fly can charge Aircraft or fight Aircraft in melee.

## Muster Your Army (pg 55-56)

### Steps:
1. **Select Battle Size** (Incursion 1000pts, Strike Force 2000pts, Onslaught 3000pts).
2. **Start Your Army Roster** (record faction, detachment, units, wargear, and points).
3. **Select Army Faction** (must have matching Faction keywords).
4. **Select Detachment Rules** (may restrict unit selection).
5. **Select Units** (at least one Character unit; consider options and upgrades).
6. **Select Warlord** (one Character unit; gains Warlord keyword).

## Missions (pg 57-60)

### Mission Play Sequence
1. Muster armies.
2. Read mission objectives.
3. Create battlefield (terrain and objectives).
4. Determine Attacker and Defender by roll-off.
5. Declare Battle Formations (attachments, embarked units, reserves).
6. Deploy armies in deployment zones.
7. Determine who goes first.
8. Resolve pre-battle rules.
9. Begin battle rounds.
10. End battle (conditions vary).
11. Determine victor (based on destruction or Victory points).

### Objective Markers
- Represent tactical assets.
- Place centered on mission-specified points.
- Models within 3" horizontally and 5" vertically are in range.
- **Level of Control:** Sum of Objective Control (OC) of models in range.
- Highest Level of Control player owns marker; ties contested.
- Models cannot end moves on objective markers.

### Object Marker & Terrain Positioning Tips
- Objective markers can be placed within terrain if stable and not overhang.
- Both players can agree to adjust positions for narrative play.

## Mission: Only War (pg 59-60)

### Overview
A classic battle to prove mastery of command by destroying the enemy and controlling strategic sites.

### Mission Summary
1. Muster armies as per battle size (Incursion, Strike Force, Onslaught).
2. Objective: Capture and Control objective markers for Victory Points.
   - From 2nd battle round onward, score 1 VP per objective held, max 3 VP per turn.
3. Create battlefield with terrain per guidelines; place 4 objective markers (not within 6" of edges or 9" of others).
4. Determine Attacker/Defender.
5. Declare battle formations secretly.
6. Deploy armies alternately within deployment zones.
7. Roll-off for first turn.
8. Resolve pre-battle rules.
9. Begin battle rounds.
10. End battle after all enemy destroyed or 5 rounds complete.
11. Determine victor by destruction or most Victory points.
