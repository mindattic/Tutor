## THE WH40K FLUFF BIBLE

---

## The Imperium

### Overview of the Imperium

The **Adeptus Mechanicus** or **Tech-Priests** are the guardians of Earth's ancient knowledge. The Adeptus Mechanicus owns and administrates the factory planet **Mars**, whose vast orbital workshops produce the majority of weapons, spacecraft, and other technologically sophisticated machinery used in the Imperium. The **Adeptus Mechanicus** is as much an arcane cult as a scientific body, and its knowledge extends into techno-arcana.

The **Administratum** is responsible for assessing and levying tithes, distributing Imperial resources, and many administrative functions. It is the largest department of the Adeptus Terra. Its members are mostly scribes and petty officials, the hereditary slaves of a galaxy-spanning bureaucracy. The Administratum’s heart lies within the Emperor's Palace, a vast complex extending underground. It is probably the most powerful organization in the entire Imperium and is divided into many departments.

The **Adeptus Terra** is also called the **Priesthood of Earth**, or simply the Adeptus. It consists of millions of dedicated servants and religious followers tasked with interpreting and enforcing the Imperial will. Adeptus Terra controls the Imperium, including its armies and fleets. It is divided into many departments and sub-departments, some operating in secrecy.

Key departments include:

- **Adeptus Ministorum (Ecclesiarchy)**: A vast, powerful organization devoted to worship of the Emperor, seen as god by billions. It controls crusading armies such as the **Adeptus Sororitas** and **Fratris**.

- **Adeptus Custodes**: The Emperor’s Guard, palace guardians charged with protecting the Imperial Palace. Only an elite inner corps, the **Companions** (300 strong), serve as the Emperor’s personal bodyguards.

- **Adeptus Astra Telepathica**: Responsible for recruiting and training **psykers** for service. This includes training **Astropaths**, psykers who can send telepathic messages over interstellar distances.

### The Imperium Now

Over ten thousand years ago, the Great Emperor of Mankind ascended the **Golden Throne of Earth**. Little record exists of the wars he waged, only the Emperor remembers. His reign marked the end of the **Age of Strife**, a period of inter-human warfare and loss of knowledge following the **Dark Age of Technology**.

The current age is marked by superstition, where technology has become intertwined with mysticism and magic. Psykers, humans with psychic abilities such as telepathy and telekinesis, are central to this era but are often feared and called **witches**.

### Note on Language

The common language of the Imperium is represented by English, with proper names anglicized. Titles of institutions are Latinized English, representing an older tongue known as "**Tech**." Tech developed during the Dark Age of Technology and carries religious and archaic significance. The common tongue varies across planets and regions.

### Note on Technology in the Age of the Imperium

Technical explanations are generally avoided to reflect the era’s brutal force and superstition. Technology is seen as almost magical, handled reverently, often with ritualistic practices. The **Adeptus Mechanicus** retains much ancient knowledge but it is somewhat corrupted.

### Transmission Systems

- **Electricity**: Primary energy form with **Stacked Atomic Chains (Stacks)** allowing molecular-level circuitry.

- **Photon lines**: Refined fibre-optics transmitting laser light via ceramic materials.

- **Phased Crystals**: Crystals that transmit signals via shape changes under heat/pressure.

- **Hydroplastics**: Transmit power via molecular-level pressure.

- **Sucrosol**: Synthetic blood feeding bioengineered parts, including robots.

- **Radio**: Similar to previous ages but narrower bands.

### Generation Systems

- **Crystal Batteries**: Engineered crystals absorbing and releasing energy, rechargeable.

- **Plasma**: Pure energy from stars, contained in magnetic fields; used mainly for drives.

- **Conventional**: Wind, tide, photo-cell, combustion, animal power on lesser worlds.

### Motors and Actuators

- **Hydraulic actuators**: Powered by hydroplastic pressure for vehicles and robots.

- **Electrically Motivated Fibre Bundles**: Contract with electric charge; used in bionics and powered armour.

- **Gravitic Reactors**: Use magnetic fields to counteract gravity; aging technology reliant on raw materials from Earth.

### Control Systems

Varied in appearance:

- **Archaic Controls**: Buttons, levers, switches, easy to understand.

- **Pure Crystal Tech**: Control panels as glass slabs floating via suspensors, activated by coded signals.

- **Holographic Projection**: 3D wrap-round visual controls sensitive to hand and eye movements.

- **Mind Impulse**: Thought-controlled systems, mostly in Dreadnought suits.

### Skinplants and Electoos

- **Skinplants**: Sophisticated tattoos with integrated electronics and light-emitting features.

- **Electoos**: Conductive layers under skin programmed for control/monitoring, used for identification.

- **Electrografts**: Implanted in cerebellum, altering memory and knowledge but may cause mental issues.

### Standard Template Constructs (STC)

Developed during the Dark Age of Technology, **STCs** are evolved computer programs providing construction blueprints for settlers. The system enabled untrained colonists to build complex machinery and structures. Over time, STCs degraded or were corrupted. The **Adeptus Mechanicus** seeks lost and functional STCs.

---

### The Imperial Fleet

Interstellar travel and transport are tightly controlled by the priesthood, especially the Adeptus Terra. The fleet consists of thousands of ships patrolling and maintaining order across the galaxy.

---

### Imperial Architecture

Using **Standard Template Construct (STC)**, most Imperial buildings follow functional designs regardless of materials like stone, brick, or coral. Types include:

- **Administratum buildings**: Headquarters of bureaucracy, identified by the Imperial eagle.

- **Auditoriums**: For public assemblies and rallies.

- **Castra Exercitus**: Barracks for planetary defense forces.

- **Chapels**: Places of worship, often targeted or desecrated by enemies.

- **Cellarion**: Warehouses for goods storage.

- **Censoriums**: Information blocks for population and economy.

- **Generatoriums**: Power supplies, from organic fuels to plasma reactors.

- **Genetoriums**: Gene-pools for flora and fauna.

- **Librariums**: Large libraries preserving knowledge.

- **Manufactoriums**: Industrial production complexes.

- **Mausoleums**: Tombs for important citizens.

- **Residential areas**: Housing varies from luxury apartments to cramped tenements.

---

### Worlds of the Imperium

There are over one million inhabited worlds, varying widely:

- **Agricultural Worlds**: Provide food for other planets.

- **Civilised Worlds**: Most common, self-sufficient and urban.

- **Death Worlds**: Hostile ecosystems with aggressive flora and fauna.

- **Feral Planets**: Societies reverted to savagery.

- **Hive Worlds**: Vast cities sprawling continents with huge populations.

- **Industrial Worlds**: Mining and manufacturing centers, often sparsely populated.

- **Medieval Worlds**: Societies with medieval technology, often isolated.

- **Paradise Planets**: Natural beauty, used as recreational bases.

- **Research Stations**: New or isolated worlds under study.

---

### The Adeptus Mechanicus

Mars is the home of the Adeptus Mechanicus, the **Tech Priests** who uphold the Cult of the Machine.

#### The Cult Mechanicus

- Worship the Emperor but reject formal Imperial cult authority.

- Regard knowledge as divine; machines and machine spirits are holy.

- Society divided between **Tech Priests** and **Servitors** (half-machine slaves).

#### Ranks in the Adeptus Mechanicus

- **Fabricator General**: Leader of the Cult and High Lord of Terra.

- **Magos**: Masters of technological fields, e.g., Technicus, Metallurgicus.

- **Logis**: Logisticians and statisticians, regarded as prophetic.

- **Genetor**: Genetic scientists.

- **Artisan**: Designers of machines and weapons.

- **Electro Priest**: Fanatical cult warriors manipulating electrical energy.

- **Engineers**: Technical experts often attached to military forces.

- **Rune Priest**: Specialists in arcane scientific rites and improvisation.

- **Transmechanic**: Communications technicians.

- **Lexmechanic**: Data analysts and archivists.

- **Servitors**: Mindless cyborg slaves performing laborious tasks.

#### The Planet Mars

- Terraformed with an atmosphere and fertile soil for industry.

- Ancient and partly ruined factory hives produce most of the Imperium’s technology.

- Orbital factories manufacture huge ships and warfleets.

#### The Titan Legions

- Massive humanoid war machines, powered by fission reactors, used mostly on Mars and forge worlds.

- Crewed by dozens or hundreds to manage weapons, propulsion, and systems.

---

### Adeptus Custodes

The Emperor's personal guard, sworn to protect him within the Imperial Palace. They never leave Earth and serve as the ultimate defenders of the Emperor.

---

### The Adeptus Arbites

The **Judges** of the Imperium, enforcing law where local governance fails, often operating with brutal efficiency. Their motto is to act as judge, jury, and executioner without recourse to trial.

---

### The Assassins

Secretive operatives tasked with covert removal of key enemies of the Imperium. Trained in disguise, poisons, and infiltration methods.

- Recruited from feral worlds.

- Use advanced drugs like **Polymorphine** and equipment such as **black syn-skin**.

- Organization led by the **Master of Assassins**, with secret headquarters on Earth.

---

### Adeptus Astra Telepathica

Responsible for recruiting, training, and managing **psykers**, particularly **Astropaths** who enable interstellar communication via telepathy.

- The psychic binding ritual called **Soul Binding** imparts enhanced powers and damages senses, causing blindness in most Astropaths.

- Astropaths have unique psychic abilities such as **Astrotelepathy**, **Locate Warp Gate**, **Psychic Beacon**, and **Warp Space Trail**.

- They wear **green robes** and typically carry staffs.

---

### Navigators

Mutant humans with the unique psychic ability to navigate through the warp safely. They possess a distinctive **Third Eye** on their forehead.

- Navigator families maintain their gene pools through intermarriage.

- Navigators do not have other psychic powers.

- Often serve aboard ships as warp pilots.

- Navigate the vast distances unreachable by standard warp travel.

---

### Rogue Traders

Licensed explorers with freedom to roam beyond the Imperium, dealing with alien races, new worlds, and unknown dangers.

- Command fleets, troops, and settlers for colonization or plunder.

- Often have complex, alien, or rare equipment.

- Due to their free status, they are considered both valuable and outcasts.

## The Galaxy of the 41st Millennium

### Worlds of the Galaxy

The galaxy is a vast spiral, approximately 90,000 light years across with 400 billion stars. Human settled worlds are scattered unevenly, with higher concentrations in galactic west and fewer in the **Eastern Fringe**.

### The Imperium

- The largest empire with over a million worlds under control.

- Governed through a vast bureaucracy called the **Adeptus Terra**.

- Interstellar travel relies on warp drives and a psychic beacon called the **Astronomican**.

### The Eastern Fringe

Border area beyond the reach of the Astronomican with fewer human worlds, many alien cultures, and significant lawlessness.

### The Eye of Terror

A massive warpstorm and area of chaos-warp-reality overlap. Refuge for Chaos forces and their surrogates. It is a dangerous place where reality is unstable.

### Wilderness Space

Unknown or unexplored zones outside settled space, often inaccessible due to warpstorms.

### Alien Worlds

Many alien races exist; most are primitive or isolated. The major adversaries of humanity include:

- **Orks**: Brutal, warlike warrior race with technology remnants.

- **Eldar**: Ancient race with advanced psychic and warp-tunnel travel.

- **Tyranids**: Hive mind bio-engineered alien enemies seeking to consume all life.

---

### Interplanetary and Interstellar Travel

- Governed by **Imperial Commanders** who hold planets on feudal terms.

- Ships without warp drives operate within star systems.

- Interstellar travel uses warp drives with complex navigation via Navigators and the Astronomican.

### The Galactic Civilisation

- The Imperium’s worlds vary in technology and culture from medieval to highly-advanced.

- Worlds are bound by law to provide troops and resources to the Imperium.

## Imperial Government

### The High Lords of Terra

The twelve most powerful officials ruling the Imperium in the Emperor's name, controlling vast armies and fleets, and directing policy.

Key positions include:

- Master of the Administratum

- Inquisitorial Representative

- Ecclesiarch of the Adeptus Ministorum

- Fabricator General of the Adeptus Mechanicus

- Grand Provost Marshal of the Adeptus Arbites

- Paternoval Envoy of the Navigators

- Master of the Astronomican

- Grand Master of the Officio Assassinorum

- Master of the Adeptus Astra Telepathica

---

### Adeptus Ministronum (Ecclesiarchy)

- The religious cult worshipping the Emperor as a god.

- Controls crusading armies (**Adepta Sororitas**).

- Missions center on persecution of mutants and heretics.

- Administered by a Holy Synod led by the **Ecclesiarch**.

---

### Administratum

Vast bureaucratic body managing the governance of the millions of Imperial worlds. Ranks from scribes to the highest ruling officials.

---

### Planetary Government

Planets are governed by **Imperial Commanders** who administer their worlds semi-autonomously under feudal obligation to the Imperium.

## Space Marines

### Overview

- Superhuman troops created by genetic engineering, training, and implants.

- Organized into 1000-man **Chapters** with their own commanders, fleets, and support staff.

- Known as the **Angels of Death** for their terrifying battlefield reputation.

- Use powered armour with enhanced strength, endurance, and coordination.

- Each chapter has unique insignia and traditions.

### History

- Originated during the Great Crusade, genetically linked to superhuman Primarchs created by the Emperor.

- Underwent 26 foundings to form new chapters over millennia.

- Gene-seed implants sometimes mutate, causing changes or deficiencies.

### Gene-Seed and Zygotes

- Nineteen artificial organs implanted in each Marine define their superhuman abilities.

- Prosthetic reproduction relies on **progenoid organs** for creating new gene-seed.

- Chapters owe gene-seed tithes to the Adeptus Mechanicus for monitoring and new chapter foundation.

### Recruitment & Risks

- Candidates must be young males with compatible tissue and mental fortitude.

- Not all survive implantation or training due to degeneration or maladaptation.

- Chemical, psychological, and hypnotic conditioning are essential.

### Space Marine Armour

- Multiple marks of armour developed over time (**Mark I – Mark VII**), ranging from early power suits to advanced forms.

- Armour valued as spiritual as well as protective gear, maintained by Marine Artificers.

---

### Space Marine Armament

- Standard weapons include **bolt guns (bolters)** firing explosive shells, **bolt pistols**, **power gloves**, and **chainswords**.

- Support weapons include heavy bolters, melta-guns, plasma guns, las-cannons, and missile launchers.

---

### Space Marine Armored Vehicles

- **Land Raider**: Main battle tank, heavily armored with lasers and bolters, transports up to ten Marines.

- **Rhino**: Common armored personnel carrier for Marines.

- **Whirlwind**: Rhino variant with missile launcher turret.

---

### The Cursed Founding (21st Founding)

- Largest since the 2nd Founding.

- Noted for genetic mutations, loss of recruits, and some chapters declared excommunicate.

---

### The Dark Angels

- Origins shrouded in mystery, with lost records.

- Founded by **Primarch Lion El'Jonson**.

- Their homeworld **Caliban** was lost during a schism caused by betrayal of Luther, second-in-command.

- The Dark Angels’ secret quest is to hunt down the **Fallen** — those brethren turned to Chaos.

---

### Deathwing

- Elite First Company of the Dark Angels.

- Monastic in nature, heavily involved in sacred rites and the secret quest for the Fallen.

---

### Leman Russ

- Primarch of the Space Wolves.

- Raised by wolves on **Fenris**.

- Fierce warrior, loyal to the Emperor.

- Known for personal rivalries and legendary feats.

- His fortress is called **The Fang** on Fenris.

---

### The Mentor Legion

- Newly founded chapter from the 26th Founding.

- Created after the disappearance of the Star Scorpions.

---

### Legion of the Damned

- Mysterious chapter associated with spectral and supernatural manifestations.

- Especially powerful, undying warriors clad in black.

---

### The Draconians

- Successors of the Salamanders.

- World of **Draconus**, volcanic and harsh.

- Specialize in fire-based tactics and weaponry.

---

### Ultramarines

- One of the largest and most successful chapters.

- Primarch **Roboute Guilliman**.

- Implemented the **Codex Astartes**, which details chapter organization.

- Known for disciplined and expansive military campaigns.

## The Horus Heresy

### Corruption of Warmaster Horus

- Horus, the favored Primarch and Warmaster, was corrupted by Chaos and led a galaxy-wide rebellion.

- Betrayal of the Emperor and the Imperium.

- Final assault on Earth led by Horus.

### Campaign Overview

- Chaos unleashed and Eldar destruction led to birth of **Slaanesh**.

- The Emperor launched the **Great Crusade**.

- Several Space Marine Legions turned traitor under Horus’ influence.

- Massive battles and internal war raged across human worlds.

- The final battle between the Emperor and Horus ends with Horus' death and the Emperor's mortal wounding.

### The Death of Sanguinius

- Primarch of the Blood Angels.

- Died facing Horus to protect the Emperor.

- His death was crucial to the Emperor's victory.

---

## The Imperial Guard

### Overview

- Represents billions of troops across many worlds.

- Engage in long or short wars across many battle zones.

- Armies recruited locally but deployed galaxy-wide.

### Structure

- Planetary Lords recruit forces, obligated to supply troops and equipment.

- Regiments can range from hundreds to hundreds of thousands strong.

- Organized with Commissars who maintain discipline and morale.

### Specialized Units

- **Storm Troopers**: Elite soldiers from best regiments.

- **Rough Riders**: Mounted infantry using horses or beasts, trained for advanced cavalry tactics.

- **Whiteshields**: Trainees and recruits yet to earn full Guardsman status.

- **Penal Legions**: Comprise criminals and undesirables, used for tough assignments.

- **Ogryn Fighters**: Large, tough abhumans with limited mental capacity but great combat strength.

- **Commissar Training Squads**: Experienced officers trained to inspire troops and maintain order.

### Battle of Armageddon

- Massive Ork incursion and siege.

- Brutal fighting with significant losses.

- Defended by famous commanders like **Commissar Yarrick**.

---

## The Inquisition

### Overview

- Powerful agents free to act across the Imperium.

- Investigate threats such as heresy, mutation, corruption, and alien influence.

### Organization

- Operate independently; respond to Imperial commands.

- Led by the **Master of the Inquisition**.

### Equipment and Uniforms

- Varied equipment, including powered armor concealed under civilian garb.

- Standard weaponry includes bolters, power swords, and digital weapons.

- Do not wear uniform; identify by badge of office when necessary.

---

### Ordo Malleus

- Secretive branch of the Inquisition.

- Tasked with protecting humanity from the forces of Chaos.

- Controls **Grey Knights**, an elite, psychic Space Marine chapter.

- Hierarchical with **Masters**, **Proctors**, and **Inquisitors**.

- Members often bear graphic electoos representing their victories.

---

### Grey Knights

- Unique among Space Marines for their knowledge of Chaos secrets.

- Perform specialized daemon-hunting duties.

- Based on **Titan**, Saturn’s moon.

- Use specialized weapons such as **Nemesis Force Weapons**.

- Training and dedication unmatched in discipline and purity.

---

## Chaos

### The Traitor Legions

Nine of the original Space Marine Legions turned to Chaos during the Horus Heresy:

- **Death Guard**: Led by Mortarion, now a Daemon Prince of Nurgle.

- **Word Bearers**: Led by Lorgar, fanatical worshipers of Chaos.

- **Night Lords**: Known for brutality and terror tactics.

- **Alpha Legion**: Secretive and conspiratorial.

- **Emperor’s Children**: Followers of Slaanesh, masters of decadence.

- **Iron Warriors**: Experts in siege warfare.

- **Thousand Sons**: Sorcerers devoted to Tzeentch.

- **Black Legion**: The former Luna Wolves, now Chaos champions under Abaddon.

- **World Eaters**: Berserkers fueling Khorne’s wrath.

### Chaos Cultists

- Infiltrate loyal populations.

- Organize into religious covens to spread Chaos.

- Worship different Chaos Gods or Chaos Undivided.

### The Eye of Terror

- Massive warp-space and material universe overlap.

- Zone of Chaos power and Daemonic influence.

- Home to Daemon Worlds ruled by Daemon Princes.

- Refuge for Chaos Cultists and renegades.

---

### Chaos Powers

- **Khorne, the Blood God**: God of rage, violence, and war.

- **Tzeentch, Changer of the Ways**: God of magic, change, and fate.

- **Nurgle, Lord of Decay**: God of plague, decay, and endurance.

- **Slaanesh, Lord of Pleasure**: God of excess, pleasure, and corruption.

---

## Alien Races

### The Eldar

- Ancient, graceful, and long-lived compared to humans.

- Once a galactic empire, collapsed due to pride and the birth of Slaanesh.

- Travel using **warp tunnels** rather than warp drives.

#### Craftworlds

- Massive spacecraft that serve as homeworlds and military bases.

- Maintain an **Infinity Circuit**, a repository of Eldar spirits.

- Use **Avatars**, living war suits powered by spirit stones.

#### Clans and Craftworlds

- Include the **Fir Lirithion, Fir Dinillainn**, **Fir Farillecassion**, **Fir Iolarion**, and others.

- Reflect various philosophies and fighting styles.

#### The Black Library

- Contain forbidden knowledge of Chaos and the warp.

- Access restricted to the **Illuminati** and **Solitaires of the Harlequins**.

#### The Harlequins

- Warrior troupe and keepers of Eldar secrets.

- Masters of performance and terror tactics.

### Eldar Exodites

- Eldar who resisted corruption and fled to live on savage worlds.

- More austere, rugged but survive better than other Eldar.

- Maintain **world spirits** for ancestral protection.

### Phantom Class Titan

- Eldar war machine emphasizing mobility and holographic defensive screens.

- Crew meld their minds with the **Infinity Circuit** for enhanced combat.

---

### The Orkoids

- Consist of **Orks**, **Gretchin**, and **Snotlings**.

- Orks are dominant, brutish warriors with a pragmatic warlike culture.

- Organized under warlords called **Nobz** and **Warlords**.

- Engage in massive military campaigns called **Waaagh!**.

- Use **Gargants**, huge war machines crewed by Orks.

- **Oddboyz** fulfill specialized roles like Mekboyz (engineers) and Weirdboyz (psyker equivalents).

- **Freebooterz** are outcast pirates and mercenaries.

- Use bizarre **Kustom weapons** and vehicles.

---

### Tyranids

- Extraterrestrial hive-mind predators invading the Imperium.

- Consist of living bio-constructs from Nom-Queens (living factory creatures).

- Use genetic adaptation to create specialized creatures like:

  - **Genestealers**: Infiltrators and shock troops.

  - **Zoats**: Ambassadors and warriors.

  - **Hunter-Slayers**: Small, fast assassins.

  - **Grabber-Slashers**: Warrior assassins with symbiotic algae.

- Tyranids create **Mind Slaves** to control bio-machines and captives.

- Hive fleets move through warp creating a **shadow in the warp** blocking communication and navigation.

- Hive Fleet **Behemoth** was the first Tyranid invasion.

- Hive Fleet **Kraken** is a more recent, devastating threat.

---

### The Squats

- Descendants of ancient human miners adapted to harsh high-gravity worlds.

- Short, sturdy, materialistic, and vengeful race.

- Organized in **Strongholds**, governed by Lords and Warlords.

- Maintain a powerful **Engineers Guild**.

- Use unique technologies including neo-plasma reactors and massive **Land Trains**.

---

### The Ancient Slann

- Possibly the oldest race in the galaxy.

- Once great empire builders and genetic experimenters.

- Reclusive, psionic amphibian race with complex tribal society.

- Warriors organized in small warbands led by warleaders and warchiefs.

- Great Mages hold highest status, aloof but powerful.

---

## The Black Library and Harlequins

- The **Black Library** contains vast forbidden knowledge about Chaos and the Eldar's fall.

- Guarded by **Guardian-Scribes**.

- **Harlequins** are traveling Eldar performers and keepers of the Black Library’s secrets.

---

## Chaos Daemon Naming

- Daemons possess **True Names**, powerful and secret.

- Use-names are adopted for identification without power significance.

- Tables exist to generate daemon true names and use-names, often long and complex.

- **Daemon Titles** vary by Chaos God, e.g., Khorne's Bloodthirsters, Slaanesh's Keepers of Secrets.
