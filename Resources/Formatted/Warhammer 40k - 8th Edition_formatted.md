## Introduction to Warhammer 40,000

Welcome to the **grim darkness of the far future!** *Warhammer 40,000* is a tabletop game where you command an army of mighty warriors. It is also a broad hobby involving collecting, building, and painting **Citadel Miniatures**. This book serves as your launching pad into all these activities and much more!

The *Warhammer 40,000* hobby opens a window into a world of excitement and adventure set in the **Dark Imperium**, where the empire of **Mankind** is beset by daemon-worshipping heretics and alien horrors. Resolute armies of the **Imperium**, from the superhuman **Space Marines** and pious **Sisters of Battle** to the vast ranks of the **Astra Militarum** and towering **Knights**, stand in their defense.

A million worlds burn with war, echoing the monstrous laughter of the **Dark Gods**.

This apocalyptic setting offers endless opportunities for collecting, building, painting, and gaming. Engagement ranges from fighting epic battles, painting display figures, to building scenic alien worlds or war machines. There is no right or wrong way to enjoy the hobby—find your passion and embrace it. 

Whether at gaming tournaments, club battles, or painting competitions, strap on your armor, load your bolter, and prepare for war!

## Collecting Citadel Miniatures

The range of **Warhammer 40,000 Citadel Miniatures** is vast and varied:

- From ground-shaking war engines to xenos swarms
- From heavily armed aircraft to courageous heroes

Grand armies surge on war-torn worlds. Infantry dig in behind barricades, **Space Marine Drop Pods** descend through the skies, artillery roars, and tanks duel through ruins. Heroes and villains clash in praise to uncaring gods.

Many hobbyists collect warriors and machines for gaming or display, assembling varied armies of heroes, vehicles, and monstrous creatures. Collections can be thematic or inspired by stories from *Games Workshop’s* novels. Building your collection grows from a handful to a mighty force, bringing pride and joy.

---

## Building and Painting

Constructing and painting **Citadel Miniatures** is a tactile, satisfying experience. Options include:

- Following instructions and guides
- Creating custom conversions and color schemes

The **Citadel Paint System** guides you from base coats and washes to highlights and special effects, producing impressive models. Most minis are unassembled and unpainted, requiring modeling tools and glue.

Painted armies bring games to life—you can almost hear tank engines roar and bolters boom. Used with **Citadel terrain**, you can create epic tabletop narratives cherished by your gaming group.

Starting can be daunting, but techniques like basecoating, washing, and drybrushing are easy to learn. Tutorials are widely available from staff, online videos, and publications.

## Warhammer 40,000 Gaming

Armies advance across tabletop battlefields telling dramatic tales of **desperate last stands**, **heroic duels**, and **cataclysmic warfare**. The universe is vast, home to many factions vying for dominance:

- The **Imperium of Man** vs **Chaos-tainted traitors** and **xenos invaders**
- Battles range from elite raids and assassination missions to epic confrontations involving millions.

War varies from dogfights and tank battles to daemon banishing. Play styles differ:

- **Open Play**: Quick, unrestricted battles with any models.
- **Narrative Play**: Story-driven games with themes and campaigns.
- **Matched Play**: Balanced, points-limited, competitive play with restrictions.

Find your style and dive into the grimdarkness.

---

### Open Play

- Most free-form, adaptable style.
- Use any models from your collection, any size armies.
- Ideal for beginners and casual games.
- Can involve multiple players.
- Emphasizes fun and creativity.

---

### Narrative Play

- Based on stories from background, novels, or your own.
- Provides story-driven scenarios and campaigns.
- Asymmetrical play, often attacker vs defender.
- Use of Battle-forged armies for faction fidelity and Command Points.
- Allows creative house rules and detailed mission objectives.

---

### Matched Play

- Structured and balanced play.
- Uses points limits to build armies.
- Requires Battle-forged armies organized into **Detachments**.
- Ideal for tournaments, leagues, and competitive play.
- Includes six **Eternal War** and six **Maelstrom of War** missions.

## The Imperium of Man

The Imperium is a sprawling human empire beset by enemies and internal strife. Key facts:

- Over 40,000 years old, founded by the **Emperor**.
- Central seat is **Holy Terra**, the cradle of humanity.
- Terribly fractured across vast distances.
- Ruled by the **High Lords of Terra**, a council interpreting the Emperor’s will.
- Besieged continuously by **xenos**, heretics, and **Chaos**.

### Lost Ages of History

- Much of human history is shrouded in legend.
- The **Age of Technology** was humanity’s golden era.
- The **Age of Strife** isolated colonies due to warp storms.
- The **Great Crusade**, led by the Emperor, reunited lost colonies.
- The **Horus Heresy** was a brutal betrayal that nearly destroyed the Imperium.
  
### The Emperor

- Once a mortal man, now kept alive by the **Golden Throne**.
- His psychic power sustains the **Astronomican**, a beacon for warp navigation.
- worshipped as a god; his rule is enforced by strict dogma.

## Forces of the Imperium

### Adeptus Astartes (Space Marines)

- Genetically enhanced superhuman warriors.
- Highly trained shock troops.
- Organized into approximately 1,000 Chapters.
- Use advanced power armor like **Mk X**.
- Spawned from **gene-seed** derived from Primarchs.
- Primaris Space Marines are new, enhanced variants.

### Astra Militarum (Imperial Guard)

- The largest imperial fighting force.
- Ordinary humans in massed regiments.
- Equipped with lasguns, tanks (e.g., **Leman Russ battle tank**), and artillery.
- Includes elite subdivisions like **Militarum Tempestus**.

### Adeptus Mechanicus

- Tech-priests of Mars and forge worlds.
- Maintain and produce technology and war machines.
- Command armies of **Skitarii** cybernetic soldiers.
- Operate powerful war constructs like **Legio Cybernetica** and **Titans**.

### Imperial Knights

- Massive bipedal walkers piloted by nobles.
- Fight in **Households** with ancient lineages.
- Bring monumental firepower to battles.

### Other

- **Adeptus Custodes**: Emperor’s personal guard.
- **Inquisition**: Secret police fighting threats.
- **Officio Assassinorum**: Elite assassins for covert missions.
- **Adepta Sororitas**: Military orders of warrior nuns.
- **Adeptus Astra Telepathica**: Psyker recruitment and training.

## Forces of Chaos

- Four **Chaos Gods**: **Khorne**, **Tzeentch**, **Nurgle**, **Slaanesh**.
- Legions of twisted **Daemons** and **Chaos Space Marines**.
- Infest the galaxy with corruption and madness.
- Seek to destroy or dominate the Imperium and reality itself.

## Playing the Game

### Core Rules Summary

- Played in battle rounds.
- Each turn has phases:
  1. Movement Phase
  2. Psychic Phase
  3. Shooting Phase
  4. Charge Phase
  5. Fight Phase
  6. Morale Phase

### Movement

- Models move up to their **Move (M)** characteristic.
- Minimum and maximum moves apply.
- Can’t move through enemies; **Flying** units ignore terrain.

### Psychic Phase

- Psykers manifest powers by passing Psychic tests.
- Opponents can attempt to **Deny Witch**.
- Powers like **Smite** cause damage or effects.

### Shooting Phase

- Units fire ranged weapons at visible enemy models.
- Weapons have types: **Assault, Heavy, Rapid Fire, Grenade, Pistol**.
- Roll dice to hit, wound, and save.

### Charge Phase

- Units can make a charge move to engage in close combat.
- Defenders may fire **Overwatch**.

### Fight Phase

- Close combat between units within 1".
- Multiple attacks resolved with hit, wound, save rolls.

### Morale Phase

- Units that lost models must test **Morale**.
- Failed tests cause models to flee.

## Advanced and Optional Rules

- Include **Battlezone effects**, **Expansions** like *Planetstrike*, *Cities of Death*, *Stronghold Assault*, and *Death from the Skies*.
- Supports narrative and campaign play styles.
- Allows multiplayer battles.

---

## Battle-forged Armies and Detachments

- Armies are organized into **Detachments**.
- Detachments have battlefield role slots: HQ, Troops, Elites, Heavy Support, Fast Attack, Flyers, Fortifications, Lord of War.
- Provides **Command Points (CP)** for Stratagems.
- Examples: Battalion, Brigade, Patrol, Vanguard.

---

## Missions and Campaigns

### Open Play Missions

- Simple, flexible gameplay.
- Examples: *Annihilation, Hold at all Costs, Death or Glory*.
- Ideal for casual or test games.

### Narrative Play

- Story-driven, recreating known battles or custom stories.
- Missions include special rules and asymmetrical roles.

### Matched Play

- Balanced competitive missions.
- Two sets: **Eternal War** and **Maelstrom of War** missions.
- Use points-based armies and battle-forged rules.

### Example Matched Play Missions

- *Retrieval Mission*
- *No Mercy*
- *The Scouring*
- *Big Guns Never Tire*
- *Secure and Control*
- *The Relic*

---

## Tactical Objectives System

- Tactical Objectives add dynamic, changing mission goals.
- Generated via dice rolls or cards.
- Players score victory points for achieving or discarding objectives.

---

## Warhammer 40,000 Environmental and Terrain Rules

- **Woods, Ruins, Barricades, Craters, Obstacles, Hills** add tactical options and cover.
- Special terrain features like **Imperial Statuary** and **Fuel Pipes** offer bonuses and hazards.
- Terrain affects movement, cover, and charge distances.

---

## Battlezones

### Night Fight

- Low visibility penalties to hit.
- Delay in reinforcements.
- Special stratagems for spotting and firing.

### Fire and Fury

- Hazardous conditions like burning skies and earth cracks.
- Meteoric debris causes mortal wounds.
- Psyker bonuses and perils.

---

## Expansions

- **Planetstrike**: Planetary invasions with orbital drops and firestorm attacks.
- **Cities of Death**: Urban warfare with building-to-building fighting.
- **Stronghold Assault**: Siege warfare with fortifications and bunkers.
- **Death from the Skies**: Aerial dogfights and bombing runs.

---

## Multiplayer and Campaign Play

### Multiplayer

- Game played between 3+ players.
- Turns and phases adapted for multiple players.
- Includes unique stratagems like **Bribe**.

### Campaigns

- Linked games with story progression.
- Types include **Tree**, **Matrix**, and **Map** campaigns.
- Develop narrative arcs and evolving army histories.

# Glossary of Key Terms

- **Adeptus Astartes:** Elite genetically enhanced super-soldiers.
- **Astra Militarum:** The main massed armies of the Imperium.
- **Citadel Miniatures:** The models used in Warhammer 40,000.
- **Daemon:** Supernatural servants of the Chaos Gods.
- **Detachments:** Groupings of units in an army.
- **Emperor:** The immortal ruler of Mankind.
- **Factions:** Distinct armies or groups in the game.
- **Power Level:** A unit's relative strength rating.
- **Psykers:** Humans with psychic abilities.
- **Stratagems:** Special tactical abilities used during the game.
- **Warp:** The alternate dimension used for faster-than-light travel; source of psychic power and Chaos.
- **Wounds:** Health points of a model.
